Why It Matters

Sound familiar?

The problem

You're losing work to operators who've never wound a toroid or reworked a BGA — because no one can tell the difference.

What NACIER does

Accreditation creates a visible, verifiable difference between a qualified industrial repairer and a generalist. Procurement teams and clients can point to your NACIER badge as the standard — and justify choosing you.

The problem

You can't get on tender panels or approved supplier lists without a formal body or industry credential behind you.

What NACIER does

NACIER accreditation is a nationally recognised credential designed to satisfy the compliance requirements of procurement teams in mining, utilities, government and large industry. The documentation is ready to go.

The problem

You're operating alone — no peers, no one to refer to or collaborate with when a job is outside your scope or geography.

What NACIER does

The NACIER member network connects repair businesses across Australia. Refer out-of-scope work to trusted peers. Take on larger or multi-site work through collaboration. Build real professional relationships.

The problem

Nobody in industry, government or enterprise even knows your sector exists as a professional discipline.

What NACIER does

NACIER is the first body to formally represent commercial and industrial repair in national industry discussions and government engagement. Your membership funds that representation — and puts your business name in those conversations.

Accreditation Standards

What "vetted" actually
means at NACIER.

NACIER accreditation is not a rubber stamp. Every applicant is reviewed by DUONS against clear professional criteria — so that businesses displaying the member badge carry real credibility.

✓
ABN Verified & Active Business
Registered Australian business in good standing
✓
Commercial or Industrial Focus
Primary operations in non-consumer electronic repair sectors
✓
Demonstrated Technical Capability
Evidenced by work history, equipment, qualifications or a combination
✓
Appropriate Insurance Coverage
Public liability minimum — professional indemnity recommended
✓
Adherence to NACIER Code of Practice
Professional conduct, accurate representation of capability, client protection

NACIER Code of Practice

Accredited members agree to uphold the following standards — the cornerstone of what makes NACIER accreditation worth something.

  • 01Represent your technical capabilities honestly and accurately to clients
  • 02Maintain appropriate insurances and comply with relevant regulations
  • 03Handle client equipment, data and documentation with due care
  • 04Support responsible repair practices and positive industry outcomes
  • 05Engage constructively with NACIER and the broader member network
  • 06Notify NACIER of material changes to business status or operations
  • 07Refrain from making misleading claims about NACIER accreditation status
